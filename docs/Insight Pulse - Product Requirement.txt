# **1. Introduction & Product Overview**

---

## **1.1 Purpose of This Document**

This document defines the **product requirements, system behavior, and user-facing structure** of **Insight Pulse**.

The purpose of this document is to:

* Specify *what the system is expected to do*
* Define *key concepts and guarantees*
* Describe *user interactions and flows*

This document intentionally **does not describe implementation details**, internal mechanisms, or technical architecture.
All descriptions are expressed in terms of **observable system behavior and outputs**.

---

## **1.2 Product Definition**

**Insight Pulse** is a **B2B market intelligence and decision-support system** designed for partner organizations to monitor, contextualize, and interpret changes in their competitive and product landscape over time.

The system:

* Operates in a **tenant-specific context**
* Surfaces **synthesized insights**, not raw information
* Emphasizes **change, patterns, and relevance** rather than static facts

Insight Pulse is designed to support **human decision-making**, not to automate or replace it.

---

## **1.3 Intended Users**

Insight Pulse is intended for users within partner organizations who are involved in:

* Strategic planning
* Product management
* Market analysis
* Competitive intelligence
* Go-to-market planning

The system assumes users:

* Have domain familiarity
* Seek prioritization and context rather than exhaustive data
* Use insights as inputs into broader decision processes

---

## **1.4 Core Value Proposition**

Insight Pulse provides value by:

1. **Reducing information overload**

   * By synthesizing signals into structured insights

2. **Highlighting meaningful change**

   * By prioritizing severity, velocity, and relevance

3. **Preserving historical context**

   * By maintaining memory of past activity and trends

4. **Linking intelligence to decisions**

   * By explicitly mapping insights to affected decision domains

The system focuses on *what matters now*, *why it matters*, and *where it may have impact*.

---

## **1.5 System Boundaries**

Insight Pulse explicitly **does not**:

* Recommend actions
* Make decisions on behalf of users
* Provide predictive guarantees
* Expose raw or unfiltered data feeds

Instead, the system guarantees:

* Contextualized presentation of intelligence
* Explainable insight generation
* Consistent behavior across tenant environments

---

## **1.6 High-Level Functional Scope**

At a high level, Insight Pulse enables users to:

* Access a centralized dashboard of market intelligence
* Track competitor-level changes and patterns
* Track product-level and adjacent product changes
* Explore insights categorized by theme, GTM, and positioning
* Identify high-priority developments through severity and velocity indicators

All functionality operates strictly within the **partner-defined scope** of products and competitors.

---

## **1.7 Compliance and Interpretability Statement**

This specification is written to be:

* **Deterministic in intent**
* **Free of ambiguous terminology**
* **Safe for interpretation by automated systems**

All key concepts used in this section are:

* Defined explicitly later in the document
* Used consistently across sections
* Independent of implementation strategy

# **2. Problem Statement & Motivation**

---

## **2.1 Context**

Organizations operating in competitive and regulated markets must continuously monitor:

* Competitor behavior
* Product-level changes
* Market-wide shifts in strategy, positioning, and go-to-market approaches

This information is **dynamic**, **fragmented**, and **uneven in relevance** across time and organizational roles.

---

## **2.2 Core Problem**

The primary problem Insight Pulse addresses is:

> **Organizations lack a structured way to continuously identify, prioritize, and contextualize meaningful market changes within their specific competitive and product scope.**

This problem manifests as difficulty in answering:

* What has changed that actually matters?
* Is this change isolated or part of a broader pattern?
* How does this change relate to our products or competitors?
* Which types of decisions does this change put pressure on?

---

## **2.3 Limitations of Existing Approaches**

Current approaches typically rely on combinations of:

* Static reports
* Periodic summaries
* Ad-hoc monitoring
* Manual interpretation of disparate information sources

These approaches have the following limitations:

1. **Lack of Continuity**

   * Information is reviewed as isolated snapshots
   * Historical context is often lost between reporting cycles

2. **Poor Signal Prioritization**

   * High-impact changes are mixed with low-relevance updates
   * Users must manually determine importance

3. **Insufficient Contextualization**

   * Changes are presented without clear linkage to:

     * Specific products
     * Competitive relationships
     * Decision relevance

4. **Reactive, Not Ongoing**

   * Insights are discovered after delays
   * Patterns emerge late, reducing response time

---

## **2.4 User Impact**

As a result of these limitations, users experience:

* Information overload without clarity
* Missed early signals of strategic change
* Difficulty justifying decisions using consistent intelligence
* Increased dependence on manual synthesis and interpretation

This increases cognitive load and reduces confidence in decision-making.

---

## **2.5 Problem Constraints**

Any solution addressing this problem must satisfy the following constraints:

* Operate within a **partner-defined scope**
* Preserve historical context across time
* Prioritize changes based on importance and momentum
* Avoid prescriptive or automated decision-making
* Provide explainable reasoning for surfaced intelligence

---

## **2.6 Motivation for Insight Pulse**

Insight Pulse is motivated by the need for a system that:

* Treats **change**, not data volume, as the primary signal
* Maintains **memory** to distinguish trends from noise
* Structures intelligence around **decision relevance**
* Supports human judgment rather than replacing it

The goal is not to increase information availability, but to **increase clarity, focus, and continuity** in how market intelligence is consumed.

---

## **2.7 Success Criteria (Problem-Oriented)**

The problem addressed by Insight Pulse is considered successfully mitigated if users can:

* Quickly identify which competitors or products have meaningfully changed
* Understand whether changes are isolated or accelerating
* See how changes relate to their own product portfolio
* Use insights as credible inputs into strategic and operational decisions

---

## **2.8 Interpretability and Compliance Statement**

This problem statement:

* Uses explicitly defined terminology
* Avoids assumptions about implementation
* Describes observable user-facing deficiencies

All terms introduced here are either defined in this document or expanded in subsequent sections, ensuring consistent interpretation by both human readers and automated systems.

# **3. Product Vision, Goals & Non-Goals**

---

## **3.1 Product Vision**

The vision of Insight Pulse is to provide partner organizations with a **continuous, structured, and explainable view of meaningful change** within their competitive and product landscape.

The system aims to enable users to:

* Maintain awareness of evolving market conditions
* Recognize emerging patterns early
* Understand how external changes relate to internal products and decisions

Insight Pulse is designed as a **decision-support system**, not a decision-making system.

---

## **3.2 Guiding Principles**

The product is guided by the following principles:

1. **Change Over Volume**
   The system prioritizes detecting and presenting change rather than aggregating large quantities of information.

2. **Context Over Raw Facts**
   Information is always presented with scope, relevance, and temporal framing.

3. **Continuity Over Snapshots**
   The system preserves historical context to distinguish trends from isolated events.

4. **Explainability Over Automation**
   All surfaced intelligence must be traceable and interpretable.

5. **Human Judgment as the Final Authority**
   The system supports, but does not replace, human decision-making.

---

## **3.3 Product Goals**

Insight Pulse has the following primary goals:

1. **Enable Continuous Situational Awareness**
   Users can quickly understand what has changed within their scoped market environment.

2. **Improve Attention Prioritization**
   The system highlights which changes are most important or urgent using severity and velocity.

3. **Preserve and Surface Historical Patterns**
   Users can recognize recurring behavior and directional trends over time.

4. **Connect Intelligence to Decision Contexts**
   Insights are explicitly mapped to the types of decisions they may influence.

5. **Reduce Cognitive Load**
   Users spend less time interpreting fragmented information and more time evaluating implications.

---

## **3.4 Success Metrics (Behavioral, Not Technical)**

The product is considered successful if users can:

* Identify high-impact changes without reviewing all updates
* Distinguish one-time events from emerging trends
* Confidently explain why a particular insight matters
* Use insights as inputs to structured decision discussions

These outcomes are evaluated through observable user behavior rather than system internals.

---

## **3.5 Explicit Non-Goals**

Insight Pulse explicitly does **not** aim to:

* Recommend specific actions
* Automate or execute decisions
* Predict future outcomes with guarantees
* Replace domain experts or strategic planning processes
* Expose raw or unfiltered information streams

Any functionality that would violate these constraints is considered out of scope.

---

## **3.6 Scope Boundaries**

To maintain focus and interpretability, Insight Pulse:

* Operates only within partner-defined products and competitors
* Surfaces insights only when relevance thresholds are met
* Avoids presenting speculative or unverifiable information

These boundaries ensure consistency, trust, and clarity across all tenant environments.

---

## **3.7 Compliance and Interpretability Statement**

This section defines **intent and constraints**, not implementation.

All goals and non-goals are:

* Expressed in observable terms
* Independent of technical design
* Used as invariants for all subsequent sections of this document

---

# **4. System Scope & Access Model**

---

## **4.1 System Scope**

Insight Pulse operates within a **strictly defined scope** determined by each partner organization.

The system scope defines:

* Which entities are visible to a user
* What types of insights may be surfaced
* How relevance and context are interpreted

All user-facing functionality is constrained by this scope.

---

## **4.2 Partner Organization Model**

Insight Pulse is accessed by **partner organizations**.

Each partner organization:

* Represents a single tenant
* Has a private, isolated view of the system
* Defines its own competitive and product universe

No partner organization has visibility into:

* Other partner organizations
* Their data
* Their insights

---

## **4.3 Tenant Definition**

A tenant is defined as:

* A logical boundary that encapsulates:

  * A set of products
  * A set of competitor organizations
  * Associated relationships and relevance rules

Tenants are **preconfigured** and available at the time of user authentication.

Tenant configuration is assumed to occur **outside the user-facing system flow**.

---

## **4.4 Product and Competitor Scope**

Within each tenant:

* The **product scope** consists of:

  * Products owned or managed by the partner organization

* The **competitor scope** consists of:

  * External organizations identified by the partner as relevant competitors

Only entities explicitly included in the tenant scope:

* Can generate insights
* Can appear in dashboards or trackers
* Can be referenced in adjacency relationships

---

## **4.5 User Access Assumptions**

Users accessing Insight Pulse:

* Belong to a single partner organization
* Are authenticated into one tenant at a time
* Share a common product and competitor scope within that tenant

Role differentiation, if any, does not affect:

* Data visibility
* Insight availability

(This may be extended in future iterations but is out of scope here.)

---

## **4.6 Access Guarantees**

Insight Pulse guarantees that:

* Users can only access data and insights within their tenant
* All surfaced intelligence is scoped to:

  * Tenant-defined products
  * Tenant-defined competitors
* No cross-tenant inference or leakage occurs

These guarantees are absolute and non-negotiable.

---

## **4.7 Scope Enforcement Principles**

The system enforces scope by:

* Ignoring entities not included in the tenant configuration
* Suppressing insights that fall outside defined relevance boundaries
* Maintaining consistency across all pages and views

Scope enforcement applies uniformly to:

* Dashboards
* Trackers
* Drill-down views

---

## **4.8 Compliance and Interpretability Statement**

This section defines **access boundaries and visibility guarantees**.

All statements are:

* Independent of authentication or storage mechanisms
* Expressed in terms of observable system behavior
* Intended to prevent ambiguity in system interpretation

Subsequent sections assume and build upon these scope constraints.

# **5. Core Concepts & Insight Model**

---

## **5.1 Purpose of This Section**

This section defines the **foundational concepts, terms, and behavioral rules** that govern how Insight Pulse represents, interprets, and presents intelligence.

All subsequent sections rely on the definitions and constraints introduced here.
No implementation details or mechanisms are assumed or implied.

---

## **5.2 Fundamental Units of Information**

Insight Pulse operates on three conceptual units. These units are logical constructs, not technical artifacts.

---

### **5.2.1 Events**

An **event** is a discrete, observable occurrence relevant to the market, a competitor, or a product.

Characteristics:

* Represents a single occurrence
* Is time-bound
* Is factual in nature

Examples:

* A competitor announces a pricing change
* A product updates its indication language
* A company enters or exits a market

Events are not surfaced directly to users.

---

### **5.2.2 Signals**

A **signal** is an interpreted indication derived from one or more events.

Characteristics:

* Represents the *type* of change implied by events
* May aggregate multiple related events
* Introduces initial context but not full interpretation

Examples:

* Increased pricing pressure
* Shift in go-to-market approach
* Expansion into an adjacent indication

Signals may persist across time and may recur.

---

### **5.2.3 Insights**

An **insight** is a synthesized interpretation of one or more signals that represents a meaningful change, emerging pattern, or risk.

Insights are the **primary unit of value** presented to users.

---

## **5.3 Formal Definition of an Insight**

> **An insight is a synthesized interpretation of one or more signals that indicates a meaningful change or pattern within a defined scope and time window, contextualized by severity, velocity, and decision relevance for a partner organization.**

This definition applies uniformly across all system views.

---

## **5.4 Required Insight Attributes**

Every insight surfaced by Insight Pulse must logically include the following attributes:

---

### **5.4.1 Scope**

Defines *what the insight applies to*:

* A specific product
* A specific competitor
* A broader thematic or market context

An insight must always have at least one clearly defined scope.

Market-Originated Insights
Insights may originate from market activity outside the explicitly defined competitor set, provided the activity has direct or adjacent relevance to a partner-defined product or market segment and meets defined severity thresholds.
Such insights are scoped to products or thematic market contexts and do not introduce new competitor entities.

---

### **5.4.2 Time Window**

Defines *when the insight emerged*:

* Expressed as a bounded period
* Supports rolling and comparative views

Time windows ensure insights are temporally grounded.

---

### **5.4.3 Severity**

Severity represents the **relative importance** of an insight.

Severity is expressed categorically (e.g., minor, notable, critical) and is determined based on factors such as:

* Nature of the change
* Breadth of impact
* Proximity to the partner’s products
* Recurrence over time

Severity is used for prioritization, not prediction.

---

### **5.4.4 Velocity**

Velocity represents the **rate and direction of change over time**.

Velocity is derived from historical context and may indicate:

* Increasing activity
* Stable behavior
* Decreasing activity

Velocity distinguishes isolated events from emerging or accelerating patterns.

---

### **5.4.5 Decision Domains**

Each insight is mapped to one or more **decision domains** indicating the types of decisions it may influence.

Examples of decision domains include:

* Pricing and contracting
* Go-to-market strategy
* Positioning and messaging
* Portfolio planning
* Competitive response planning

This mapping provides relevance without prescribing actions.

---

## **5.5 Temporal Memory and Continuity**

Insight Pulse explicitly maintains **memory across time**.

This enables the system to:

* Detect recurrence
* Compare current activity to historical baselines
* Surface trends rather than isolated changes

Temporal memory is used to:

* Inform velocity
* Provide historical context
* Support longitudinal analysis

The system treats time as a first-class dimension of intelligence.

---

## **5.6 Adjacency and Relationship Modeling**

Insight Pulse defines **adjacency** to explain indirect relevance.

---

### **5.6.1 Adjacency Dimensions**

Two entities are considered adjacent if they overlap along one or more declared dimensions, such as:

* Therapeutic area
* Indication
* Modality or mechanism
* Target segment
* Pricing or reimbursement pathway

Adjacency is declarative and explainable.

---

### **5.6.2 Adjacency Rule**

> A change affecting an entity is considered adjacent if the entity shares at least one defined adjacency dimension with a partner-scoped product.

All adjacency-based insights must explicitly state the dimension involved.

---

## **5.7 Insight Categorization**

Insights are categorized to support structured interpretation.

Primary categories include:

* Theme insights (market-wide patterns)
* GTM insights (market approach and execution)
* Positioning insights (messaging and differentiation)

While most insights are categorized as Theme, GTM, or Positioning, the system may surface high-severity or high-velocity insights that do not yet map cleanly to a single category.
Such insights are considered uncategorized insights and are eligible for inclusion in overview-level presentations.

Categorization does not alter insight meaning; it supports navigation and analysis.

---

## **5.8 Explainability and Consistency Guarantees**

Insight Pulse guarantees that:

* Every insight is traceable to underlying signals
* Relevance and adjacency are explicitly stated
* Severity and velocity are applied consistently
* Similar patterns produce comparable insights across time

Explainability is a core requirement, not an optional feature.

---

## **5.9 Constraints and Invariants**

The following constraints apply to all concepts defined in this section:

* Insights must not be speculative
* Insights must not recommend actions
* Insights must be scoped to the tenant context
* Insights must be interpretable without system internals

These constraints are enforced uniformly across the system.

---

## **5.10 Interpretability Statement**

All concepts defined in this section:

* Are used consistently across the document
* Are independent of implementation choices
* Serve as invariants for system behavior

Any future extension of Insight Pulse must preserve these definitions unless explicitly revised.

# **6. Intelligence Presentation & Prioritization**

---

## **6.1 Purpose of This Section**

This section defines **how insights are presented to users** and **how user attention is guided** across the system.

The goal is to ensure that:

* High-impact intelligence is surfaced prominently
* Lower-priority information does not overwhelm users
* Users can move from overview to detail in a controlled manner

This section describes *presentation logic*, not visual layout.

---

## **6.2 Presentation Principles**

All intelligence in Insight Pulse is presented according to the following principles:

1. **Priority Before Exhaustiveness**
   Users are shown what matters most before what is merely recent.

2. **Context Before Detail**
   Summaries and indicators precede drill-down information.

3. **Continuity Over Snapshots**
   Insights reflect patterns over time, not isolated moments.

4. **Consistency Across Views**
   The same insight maintains its meaning and priority wherever it appears.

---

## **6.3 Attention Prioritization Framework**

Insight Pulse uses **severity** and **velocity** as the primary mechanisms for guiding user attention.

### **6.3.1 Severity-Based Prioritization**

* Insights with higher severity are surfaced more prominently
* Severity communicates *importance*, not urgency
* Severity is used to:

  * Order insights
  * Determine inclusion in summary views

---

### **6.3.2 Velocity-Based Prioritization**

* Insights with increasing velocity indicate accelerating change
* Velocity communicates *momentum*
* Velocity is used to:

  * Highlight emerging patterns
  * Differentiate trends from one-off changes

---

### **6.3.3 Combined Interpretation**

When severity and velocity are considered together:

* High severity + high velocity → immediate attention
* High severity + low velocity → strategic monitoring
* Low severity + high velocity → early signal
* Low severity + low velocity → background context

This interpretation is implicit and non-prescriptive.

---

## **6.4 Overview-Level Intelligence Presentation**

The system provides **high-level summaries** that allow users to rapidly assess the current state of their environment.
Overview-level presentations may include uncategorized insights when immediacy or significance outweighs the need for structured classification.

These summaries:

* Aggregate insights meeting defined significance thresholds
* Exclude low-relevance information by default
* Preserve links to deeper exploration

Overview-level presentation answers:

> “What should I be paying attention to right now?”

---

## **6.5 Rolling Pulse Presentation**

The Rolling Pulse is a key presentation construct.

Characteristics:

* Represents insights observed over a rolling time window
* Includes only insights meeting significance criteria
* Emphasizes accumulation and persistence of change

The Rolling Pulse enables users to:

* See how recent changes relate to one another
* Understand short-term evolution without reviewing individual updates

---

## **6.6 Categorized Insight Presentation**

Insights are grouped into conceptual categories to support structured interpretation:

* **Theme Insights**
  Surface market-wide or cross-entity patterns.

* **GTM Insights**
  Surface changes in market engagement or execution strategies.

* **Positioning Insights**
  Surface shifts in messaging, differentiation, or target focus.

Categorization aids navigation but does not alter insight priority.
Theme insights may include high-severity market signals originating outside the defined competitor set when they materially affect the partner’s product landscape.

---

## **6.7 Drill-Down Readiness**

All summarized intelligence must support **progressive disclosure**.

This means:

* Every summary view has a corresponding detailed view
* Users can access deeper context without losing orientation
* Historical and relational context is preserved during exploration

Drill-down is optional, not required, for understanding.

---

## **6.8 Consistency Across Entry Points**

The same insight may appear in multiple contexts (e.g., dashboard, product view, competitor view).

Insight Pulse guarantees that:

* The insight’s meaning does not change across contexts
* Severity, velocity, and scope remain consistent
* Presentation adapts only in framing, not substance

---

## **6.9 Non-Prescriptive Framing**

All intelligence is presented as **contextual information**, not instruction.

The system:

* Does not suggest actions
* Does not rank “recommended responses”
* Does not imply correctness of any decision

Presentation is designed to support judgment, not replace it.

---

## **6.10 Interpretability and Compliance Statement**

This section defines **observable presentation behavior**.

All prioritization and grouping rules:

* Are independent of computation methods
* Can be interpreted without system internals
* Are applied uniformly across the system

These rules ensure predictable and explainable user experience.

# **7. User Interaction & Page Architecture**

This section describes the user-facing flow of Insight Pulse, starting from onboarding through core functional pages.
It is **UI-agnostic but element-specific**, defining *what must exist* on each page without constraining layout or visual positioning.

---

## **7.1 Onboarding Page**

### **7.1.1 Purpose**

The onboarding page introduces Insight Pulse to first-time users and establishes:

* What the system does
* What kind of value it provides
* How users will interact with it once authenticated

This page exists **before authentication** and does not expose any tenant-specific data.

---

### **7.1.2 Core Elements**

The onboarding page must include:

1. **Product Overview**

   * A concise explanation of Insight Pulse as a B2B market intelligence and decision-support system
   * Emphasis on:

     * Continuous monitoring
     * Synthesized insights (not raw data)
     * Support for strategic and operational decision-making

2. **What Insight Pulse Is Used For**

   * High-level description of:

     * Tracking market and competitor activity
     * Monitoring product and adjacent product changes
     * Understanding GTM, positioning, and thematic shifts over time

3. **Access Clarification**

   * Clear indication that Insight Pulse is:

     * Available only to partner organizations
     * Accessed via company-provisioned credentials

4. **Call-to-Action**

   * Option to proceed to login using company credentials
   * Optional secondary CTA (e.g., contact or request access), without functional dependency

---

### **7.1.3 Transition**

* When the user chooses to log in, they are taken to the **Login Page**
* No tenant creation or configuration occurs on the frontend

---

## **7.2 Login Page**

### **7.2.1 Purpose**

The login page authenticates users into their **preconfigured tenant environment**.

All tenant setup (products, competitors, access rules) is assumed to be completed **backend-side**.

---

### **7.2.2 Core Elements**

1. **Credential-Based Authentication**

   * Login using company-provided credentials
   * No sign-up or tenant configuration flows

2. **Context Awareness**

   * Authentication is mapped to:

     * A specific partner organization
     * A predefined tenant scope

3. **Error Handling**

   * Invalid credentials or access issues are handled without exposing system internals

---

### **7.2.3 Transition**

* On successful authentication, the user is routed directly to the **Dashboard**
* The dashboard reflects only the authenticated tenant’s scope

---

## **7.3 Dashboard**

### **7.3.1 Purpose**

The dashboard is the **central intelligence hub** of Insight Pulse.
It provides a high-level, continuously updated view of the partner’s market and competitive landscape.

The dashboard is designed for:

* Rapid situational awareness
* Prioritization of attention
* Entry points into deeper exploration

---

### **7.3.2 Core Elements (Position-Agnostic but Mandatory)**

#### **7.3.2.1 Recent Market Activity**

* A section summarizing the most recent, high-signal events and insights
* Focuses on:

  * Meaningful changes
  * Emerging patterns
  * High-severity or high-velocity activity

This section answers:

> “What has changed recently that I should be aware of?”

---

#### **7.3.2.2 Rolling Pulse (formerly Weekly Brief)**

* A summarized view of key insights over a rolling time window
* Window size is predefined (e.g., last 7–14 days)
* Includes:

  * Only insights meeting significance criteria (severity or velocity)

This section emphasizes:

* Continuity over time
* Change accumulation rather than isolated events

---

#### **7.3.2.3 General Insights**

The dashboard includes three **distinct but conceptually aligned insight categories**:

1. **Theme Insights**

   * Market-wide or macro-level patterns
   * Examples:

     * Emerging therapeutic trends
     * Shifts in regulatory or technology focus

2. **GTM (Go-To-Market) Insights**

   * Changes in how companies are approaching the market
   * Examples:

     * Channel strategy changes
     * Sales model evolution
     * Market entry or exit behavior

3. **Positioning Insights**

   * Changes in how products or companies position themselves
   * Examples:

     * Messaging shifts
     * Target segment changes
     * Differentiation claims

Each insight:

* Is synthesized (not raw data)
* Carries severity and velocity metadata
* Is scoped to the tenant’s products or competitors

---

### **7.3.3 Navigation**

The dashboard provides access to:

* Competitor Tracker
* Product Tracker

---

## **7.4 Competitor Tracker**

### **7.4.1 Purpose**

The competitor tracker allows users to monitor **partner-defined competitor companies**, identify recent changes, and drill into detailed competitive intelligence.

---

### **7.4.2 Competitor List View**

#### **7.4.2.1 Core Elements**

1. **Competitor List**

   * Displays all competitors configured for the tenant
   * Each competitor is represented as a distinct list item

2. **Status Indicators**

   * Visual markers indicating whether:

     * New information has been synced recently
     * Significant updates have occurred since last review

3. **Quick Update Summary**

   * Brief, high-level description of recent activity (if any)
   * Designed for scanning, not deep analysis

This view answers:

> “Which competitors have changed, and where should I look first?”

---

### **7.4.3 Expanded Competitor View**

Triggered when a user selects a competitor from the list.

#### **7.4.3.1 Core Elements**

1. **Detailed Insights**

   * Synthesized insights specific to the selected competitor
   * Organized by:

     * Theme
     * GTM
     * Positioning
     * Product-level changes (where relevant)

2. **Temporal Context**

   * Insights reflect historical memory:

     * Recurrence
     * Trend direction
     * Velocity

3. **Explainability**

   * Each insight is traceable to underlying signals
   * Adjacency or relevance is explicitly stated where applicable

This view supports:

* Strategic analysis
* Competitive planning
* Longitudinal understanding of competitor behavior

---

## **7.5 Product Tracker**

### **7.5.1 Purpose**

The product tracker focuses on the **partner’s own products** and monitors both direct and adjacent market activity affecting them.

---

### **7.5.2 Product List View**

#### **7.5.2.1 Core Elements**

1. **Product List**

   * Displays all products configured for the tenant

2. **Update Indicators**

   * Visual markers showing:

     * Recent updates to the product itself
     * Relevant changes in adjacent products

3. **Quick Update Signals**

   * Brief indicators of:

     * What changed
     * Whether the change is direct or adjacent

This view answers:

> “Which of our products are affected by recent market or competitive changes?”

---

### **7.5.3 Expanded Product View**

Triggered when a user selects a product from the list.

#### **7.5.3.1 Core Elements**

1. **Product-Specific Insights**

   * Insights scoped directly to the selected product
   * Includes:

     * Market changes
     * Competitor actions
     * Adjacent product movements

2. **Adjacency Context**

   * Explicit explanation of:

     * Why a change is considered adjacent
     * Which adjacency dimension applies

3. **Temporal and Priority Signals**

   * Severity and velocity indicators
   * Historical recurrence where applicable

This view supports:

* Product strategy
* Risk assessment
* Opportunity identification

---

## **7.6 Cross-Cutting Design Principles**

Across all pages:

* **No raw data dumps**
